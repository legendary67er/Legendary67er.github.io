Exhibit Introduction

This exhibit shows five military artifacts from ancient civilizations. Each object demonstrates how early societies used weapons, armor, and technology to protect themselves, conquer enemies, and display power.

Assyrian Battering Ram Mesopotamia

Ancient Assyria • Siege Weapon

The Assyrian battering ram was a powerful siege weapon designed to break through fortified city walls and gates. It was mounted on wheels and often protected by wooden armor so soldiers could operate it safely under enemy fire. This weapon shows how Assyria combined engineering, discipline, and military organization to expand its empire.

Hittite War Chariot Anatolia

Hittite Empire • Chariot Warfare

The Hittite war chariot was a fast and deadly battlefield weapon. Crewed by multiple soldiers, it allowed armies to strike quickly, disrupt enemy formations, and control large areas of the battlefield. Chariots show how speed and coordination were key to Bronze Age warfare.

Spartan Helmet Greece

Sparta • Hoplite Armor

The Spartan helmet was made of bronze and designed for close combat. It protected the face and head while reinforcing the disciplined, formation-based fighting style of Greek hoplites. The helmet symbolizes Spartan military culture and devotion to warfare.

Terracotta Army Soldier China

Qin Dynasty • Imperial Army

These life-size clay soldiers were buried with China’s first emperor to guard him in the afterlife. The Terracotta Army represents the size, discipline, and authority of Qin military power and imperial control.

Egyptian Khopesh Sword Egypt

New Kingdom Egypt • Curved Sword

The khopesh was a curved sword used by Egyptian soldiers and pharaohs. Its hooked shape made it effective for slashing and pulling enemy weapons aside. It also symbolized royal power and military success.
